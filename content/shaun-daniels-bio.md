# About

Shaun Alexander Daniels, MBA, is an analytics and business process strategist who builds the operational infrastructure that makes decisions visible, defensible, and repeatable. With a career spanning enterprise operations, commercial strategy, and independent consulting, Shaun has consistently done the same thing across every context: found what the data was hiding and built the system to surface it.

Shaun was educated in New Haven, Connecticut, at the Foote School and Hopkins School, one of the top fifty independent day schools in the country. He pursued Electrical and Computer Engineering at Temple University before entering the workforce early, and earned his MBA at Albertus Magnus College in a program where Yale faculty taught theory and Fortune 500 executives taught applied practice in the second course of every subject. That education instilled a discipline he carries into every engagement: understand the framework, then understand how decisions actually get made.

His professional record reflects that discipline in measurable terms. Early in his career, Shaun built a revenue-per-hour metric at the employee-hour grain that exposed a scheduling blind spot invisible at the shift level. Reallocating based on that model produced a 54% increase in revenue. He later originated the concept for a first-to-market remote diagnostic tool in the healthcare technology space and built the financial model that made the commercial case for it. Working hand in hand with clinical, commercial, and executive stakeholders from concept through beta testing, he ensured the product was market-ready at launch, contributing to a landmark venture and strategic funding milestone.

Through MHG Strategy, his consulting practice, Shaun has spent the past five years solving problems that required building from scratch. He has designed replacement financial reporting systems that restored executive visibility during high-growth periods when operating blind was not an option. He has built driver-based forecasting models that identified uncaptured demand and lifted revenue 67% from what leadership believed was their ceiling. He has run financial reconciliations that disproved costly assumptions, redirected misguided interventions, and delivered outcomes that stuck: in one engagement, a unified inventory system reduced waste by 70%.

Shaun has relocated to California to work with organizations that are serious about using data to operate better and grow faster. His focus is the work that matters regardless of industry: building the analytical infrastructure, digitizing processes that currently live in people's heads, and upgrading how leadership acts on the information it already has. The companies that move decisively on that build now, whether in manufacturing, healthcare, logistics, hospitality, or any sector where decisions still outpace the data behind them, are the ones he is here to work with.
