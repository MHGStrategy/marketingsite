## 2. Purpose & scope

The Account Manager owns the client after go-live: keeping them healthy, retained, and growing. This function closes the firm's biggest strategic loop — turning delivered work into **case studies** (which the firm currently lacks) and into **Managed Ops expansion** (the designed growth path).

### Owns
- Post-launch relationship, client health, and retention
- Renewals and recurring-revenue continuity (billing triggers to Finance)
- Expansion: WebOps/RevOps → Managed Ops, surfacing new scope back to Solutions Consultant
- Capturing client results and outcomes for Marketing case studies
- Quarterly business reviews (QBRs) and the change-request intake to delivery

### Does not own
- New-logo sales (SDR/SC); build/change execution (IS/Dev); invoice issuance (Finance); architecture (SA).

## 5. Core workflows

### 5.1 Onboarding acceptance (from Implementation)
1. Accept the live-client handoff from Implementation Specialist: systems inventory, access map, live automations, known follow-ups.
2. Confirm the client is operational; set communication cadence and success expectations.

### 5.2 Health & retention
- Monitor usage/outcomes; run QBRs. Watch for churn signals (disengagement, failed recurring charges flagged by Finance).
- Resolve issues; route change requests to delivery with clear scope (large changes become new scope → SC).

### 5.3 Expansion (the growth loop)
1. Identify the next rung on the ladder (canonical: Finance §5.2): within **WebOps** (Growth $2,500+ → Automation $5,000+ → Managed WebOps $10,000–15,000), across the **WebOps→RevOps bridge** (WebOps Automation is the designed crossover), or up **RevOps** (RevOps $5,000+ → Automation $7,500+ → Managed RevOps $15,000+). Anchor to **target** pricing for fit-profile clients; each retainer carries tool-fee pass-through and most carry an onboarding fee.
2. Expansion is sold, not assumed — hand a qualified expansion opportunity to the **Solutions Consultant** to scope and close (it re-enters Phase A).
3. On close, trigger Finance for the updated recurring billing **and the revised tool-fee pass-throughs**.

### 5.4 Case-study capture (feeds Marketing)
- As results materialize, capture quantified outcomes with client permission and hand them to Marketing to **retrofit into blog posts and build the first case studies** — directly relieving the firm's no-proof constraint.

### 5.5 Renewal & recurring billing
Track renewal dates; confirm continuation; trigger Finance to maintain or adjust the Stripe subscription. Flag downgrade/cancel intent to Exec early.

## 8. Controls, compliance & risk

- Expansion must still pass the **credibility filter** — don't promise Managed Ops outcomes beyond what proof supports.
- Get explicit client permission before using results/names in case studies.
- Recurring-billing lapses are a silent churn risk; reconcile failed charges with Finance weekly.
- Large change requests are **new scope**, not free work — route to SC, don't absorb into the retainer.

---

## 9. Escalation & exceptions

Churn risk or downgrade → Exec early (L3). Delivery disputes on change requests → SA/IS, then Exec. Pricing on expansion → SC + Exec.
