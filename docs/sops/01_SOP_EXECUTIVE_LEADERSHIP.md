## 2. Purpose & scope

Executive Leadership sets direction and holds the firm's credibility line. Every positioning claim, price, and partnership passes through this function's judgment.

### Owns
- Strategy, vision, and the go-to-market thesis (two front doors: WebOps for SMB, RevOps for mid-market; Managed Ops as expansion)
- Pricing authority, discount approval, and packaging
- Positioning and brand standards — the **credibility filter**: no claim ships without proof to support it
- Partnerships (e.g., the Boston channel partner) and commission-structure terms
- Hiring, role definition, and the sequencing of this SOP set into individual roles
- Capital allocation, banking relationships, and final financial control approval
- L3 escalation for every other function

### Does not own
- Day-to-day campaign creative (Marketing), deal execution (SDR/SC), build work (SA/IS/Dev), invoice mechanics (Finance), or account servicing (AM) — Exec governs, it does not operate these.

## 5. Core workflows

### 5.1 Pricing & discount authority
Exec sets and owns the rate card (operationalized by Finance §5.2). Current published floor:

| Tier | Floor → Target | Onboarding |
|------|----------------|------------|
| WebOps GTM | $600+ | — |
| WebOps Growth | $2,500+ | — |
| WebOps Automation | $5,000+ | $5,000 |
| Managed WebOps | $10,000–15,000 (by qualification) | $10,000 |
| RevOps Consult | $2,500 → $3,000/mo ($250/hr virtual · $500/hr in-person + travel) | — |
| RevOps | $5,000 → $6,000/mo + tool fees | $5,000 |
| Automation | $7,500 → $10,000–12,000/mo + tool fees | $10,000 |
| Managed RevOps | $15,000 → $18,000–25,000/mo + tool fees | — |

Project builds over $1,500: 40% non-refundable upfront / 30% at 50% / 30% at 85% (milestone billing above ~$25k — proposed). Quote at **target**; hold **floor** as walk-away.

1. SC quotes within this card; the **only** standing discount is **50% off WebOps** for non-profits/churches/community activists.
2. Any discount beyond that floor routes to Exec (L3) before the agreement is sent.
3. Exec records the rationale — discounting without proof erodes the credibility position.

### 5.2 Positioning / claim approval (credibility filter)
1. Marketing or SC proposes a claim (stat, case study framing, tagline).
2. Exec validates against available proof. **Rule:** no case studies yet → claims rely on third-party data and argument quality, not invented results. Statistics must be source-verified.
3. Approved claims enter the shared collateral library; rejected ones are reframed qualitatively.

### 5.3 Partnership & commission decisions
1. Finance/Marketing draft commission options (bounty, lifetime MRR %, hybrid).
2. Exec selects and resolves open terms — including the **termination-clause treatment of lifetime MRR commissions** (open decision point).
3. Finance operationalizes payouts; Marketing manages the relationship.

## 8. Controls, compliance & risk

- **Credibility risk** is the firm's signature risk: every public claim is proof-gated. (JD/MBA standard.)
- All client-facing documents use the legal entity **Mikel Hunt Group Inc. DBA MHG Strategy**, 712 Congress Avenue, New Haven, CT 06519; default governing law Connecticut.
- Exec is the sole approver of financial controls and the second signer on material commitments.
- *This SOP is operational guidance, not legal or financial advice; agreements and tax positions are reviewed with licensed counsel/CPA as the firm scales.*
