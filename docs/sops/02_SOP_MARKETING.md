## 2. Purpose & scope

Marketing creates qualified demand and protects the brand's credibility while doing it. Because the firm has **no published case studies yet**, marketing wins on argument quality and verified third-party data, not on social proof it doesn't have.

### Owns
- Content/blog campaign (indirect marketing series), SEO, and AI-search readiness
- Website conversion copy and structure (two-card conversion router; WebOps and RevOps landing pages; Managed Ops; About)
- Brand assets, tagline ("AI-Powered Ops"), and the offers campaign creative
- Channel-partnership relationship (Boston marketing company) and co-marketing
- Demand-gen channels and campaign performance reporting

### Does not own
- Lead qualification or outreach execution (SDR), deal collateral *in-deal* (SC uses it), commission economics (Finance/Exec), or site build (Developer). Marketing writes copy and requests builds; it does not deploy code.

## 5. Core workflows

### 5.1 Content campaign (standing series)
- Posts run **~750 words**, avoid unverifiable statistics, and carry verified CTAs to `/webops` (SMB) or `/revops` (mid-market) by audience.
- **Source rigor:** hard sources (primary research) and soft sources (aggregator benchmarks) are labeled in footers; unverifiable folklore stats are converted to qualitative claims. Google documentation anchors WebOps content.
- Posts are **retrofitted with real client results** as they become available (see AM interface).
- **Pricing anchors for landing copy** (canonical: Finance §5.2): publishable WebOps ladder — GTM **$600+**, Growth **$2,500+**, Automation **$5,000+**; the **50% non-profit/church/community-activist** WebOps discount is a positioning asset for mission-driven segments. **Do not publish RevOps tier numbers or either Managed tier** without Exec sign-off (RevOps is quote-on-a-call; Managed is by qualification).
- **"Fractional" framing** belongs in RevOps/editorial contexts, not SMB WebOps copy. Attach it to the service model ("fractional ops"), not to an executive title.

| Post | Audience | Status |
|------|----------|--------|
| #1 Website conversion teardown | WebOps / SMB | Complete |
| #2 Fractional ops vs. full-time hire math | RevOps / mid-market | Queued |
| #3 AI search readiness | WebOps / SMB | Planned |
| #4 Contrarian service-line focus | Editorial | Planned |
| #5 AI agents data-readiness | RevOps / mid-market | Planned |

### 5.2 Conversion-gap check (run on every launch)
Recurring failure mode: a CTA points to a page whose copy/structure doesn't echo the framing that brought the visitor. **Before any blog or email goes live, verify the destination page echoes the entry framing.** Open flag: a RevOps "fractional" landing page is needed to receive Blog #2; absence is a live conversion gap.

### 5.3 Channel partnership
Manage the Boston partner relationship and co-marketing for WebOps referrals; Finance/Exec own commission economics and payouts.

## 8. Controls, compliance & risk

- **Every statistic verified before use**; sources labeled. This is a standing constraint, not a per-post step.
- No claim ships without Exec credibility-filter sign-off.
- Public copy excludes segment labels ("Small Business," "Mid-Market") to avoid disqualifying prospects who don't self-identify.
- No FinOps or Enterprise positioning on public pages (dropped for lack of proof).
